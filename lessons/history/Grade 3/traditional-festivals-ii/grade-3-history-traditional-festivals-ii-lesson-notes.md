# Lesson Notes

**Class:** Grade 3

**Subject:** History

**Topic:** Traditional Festivals II

## Lesson Content
Nigeria has many famous traditional festivals. We learned about the New Yam Festival and Durbar Festival. Today, we will learn about more festivals.

## Argungu Fishing Festival
The Argungu Fishing Festival is celebrated in Kebbi State, Northern Nigeria. It takes place in the town of Argungu. Men throw nets into the river to catch fish. The biggest fish wins a prize. This festival has been celebrated for many years. It brings joy and shows people's fishing skills.

## Osun-Osogbo Festival
The Osun-Osogbo Festival is celebrated in Osogbo, Osun State. It is a religious festival for the Yoruba people. It is held in honour of the river goddess, Osun. People wear white clothes and dance to the river. The festival lasts for two weeks. It attracts people from all over the world.

## Eyo Festival (Lagos)
The Eyo Festival is celebrated in Lagos State. It is also called the "Eyo Festival" or "Adamu Orisha Play". Only men take part in this festival. They wear white hats and climb ladders. The festival shows Lagos history and culture. It is a time of joy and celebration for the people of Lagos.

## Unity Through Festivals
Festivals bring people together from different places. They help us understand each other's cultures. When we celebrate together, we forget our differences. Festivals teach us to live in peace and harmony. They help strengthen friendship among Nigerians.
