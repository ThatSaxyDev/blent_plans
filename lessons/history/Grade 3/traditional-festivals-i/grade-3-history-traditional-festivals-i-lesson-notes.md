# Lesson Notes

**Class:** Grade 3

**Subject:** History

**Topic:** Traditional Festivals I

## Lesson Content
Nigeria has many different festivals. These festivals are part of our rich culture and traditions.

## New Yam Festival (Igbo)
The New Yam Festival is celebrated by the Igbo people. It is also called "Igbo" or "Iri Ji". It is celebrated in August or September when the new yam is harvested. The festival gives thanks to God for a good harvest. Families prepare yam dishes and share with neighbours. Old yams are thrown away to make room for the new ones.

## Durbar Festival (Northern Nigeria)
The Durbar Festival is celebrated in Northern Nigeria, especially in Kano, Katsina, and Zaria. It marks the end of the fasting month of Ramadan and also celebrates Eid. Horsemen ride through the streets in colourful clothes. The Emir leads the procession. It shows respect for the leaders and unity among the people.

## Purpose and Significance
Festivals have important meanings. They help us remember our history and traditions. They give thanks for good things like harvest, health, and peace. They also teach younger generations about their culture.

## How Festivals Bring People Together
Festivals bring people together in many ways. Families come together to celebrate. Neighbours share food and joy. People forget their differences and unite as one. Festivals create happiness and strengthen bonds between people.
