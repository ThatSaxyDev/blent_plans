# Lesson Notes

**Class:** Grade 5

**Subject:** History

**Topic:** Democracy and Good Governance

## Lesson Content
Democracy is a system of government where the people elect their leaders and participate in decision-making. It is built on the principle that power belongs to the people, and leaders must serve the interests of the citizens who elected them. In a democracy, everyone has rights and responsibilities that ensure the government works for the good of all.

Good governance goes hand in hand with democracy. It means that leaders are honest, accountable, and transparent in how they use public resources and make decisions. When leaders are accountable, they must answer for their actions. When government is transparent, citizens can see how decisions are made and how money is spent.

## Key Points
Democracy has several core principles that make it work for everyone. First, there is freedom of speech, which means people can express their opinions openly without fear. Second, there is equality, meaning every person's vote counts the same. Third, in elections, all adults have the right to vote, and the voting process is secret to protect people's choices. Fourth, the principle of majority rule with minority rights ensures that while the majority decides, the rights of minorities are protected.

Nigeria practices democracy through three arms of government that work together. The Executive arm is led by the President and Governors, and their job is to enforce laws. The Legislature is made up of elected representatives who make laws for the country. The Judiciary interprets the laws and ensures justice is served in courts. Each arm checks the others to prevent abuse of power.

The role of citizens is vital in a democracy. Citizens vote during elections to choose their leaders. They attend town hall meetings to discuss community issues. They report problems like bad roads or corruption to appropriate authorities. Citizens also obey laws and pay taxes, which helps the government function. Most importantly, citizens must demand good governance and hold their leaders accountable.
