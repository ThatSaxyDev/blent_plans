# Lesson Notes

**Class:** Grade 4

**Subject:** History

**Topic:** States Creation in Nigeria II

## Lesson Content
We learned that Nigeria started with 12 states in 1967. Over time, more states were created to make governance easier.

## 30 States (1991)
In 1991, more states were created. This brought the total to 30 states. The creation allowed better administration and development in different areas.

## 36 States (1996)
In 1996, six more states were created. This brought the total to 36 states. This is the number of states we have today.

## Current States and Their Capitals
Nigeria has 36 states. Some key states and capitals are:
- Lagos State - Lagos
- Kano State - Kano
- Rivers State - Port Harcourt
- Delta State - Asaba
- Oyo State - Ibadan
- Anambra State - Awka
- Edo State - Benin City
- Akwa Ibom State - Uyo

## Memorizing States and Capitals
Here are some tips to memorize:
- Group states by region (North, South, East, West)
- Learn states in alphabetical order
- Use songs or rhymes
- Practice with maps and flashcards

## Local Government Creation
Local governments are the smallest tier of government. They handle local issues like roads, markets, and sanitation. Nigeria has 774 local government areas. They were created to bring government closer to the people.
