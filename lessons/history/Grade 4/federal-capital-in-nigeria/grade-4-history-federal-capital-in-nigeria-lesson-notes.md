# Lesson Notes

**Class:** Grade 4

**Subject:** History

**Topic:** Federal Capital in Nigeria

## Lesson Content
Every country has a capital city where the government is based. Nigeria's capital has changed over time.

## Lagos as Federal Capital (1914-1991)
Lagos was Nigeria's capital for many years. It became the capital in 1914 when the Southern and Northern protectorates were joined. Lagos was the centre of trade and government. It remained the capital for 77 years until 1991.

## Reasons for Moving the Capital
Lagos had some problems:
- It was too crowded
- There was not enough space for growth
- Traffic was very heavy
- It was in the southern part of Nigeria

The government wanted a more central location.

## Selection of Abuja
Abuja was chosen as the new capital in 1991. It is located in the middle of Nigeria. This makes it fair for all regions. The choice was made by the federal government to promote unity.

## Development of Abuja
Abuja was built from scratch. Modern buildings were constructed. Roads were laid out. The city was planned with different areas. It became a beautiful and organised city.

## Abuja as FCT
Abuja is not a state. It is called the Federal Capital Territory (FCT). The President lives and works in Abuja. It belongs to all Nigerians.
