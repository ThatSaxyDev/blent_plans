# Lesson Plan

**Class:** Grade 4

**Subject:** History

**Duration:** 40 minutes

**Gender:** Mixed

**Topic:** Federal Capital in Nigeria

**Subtopics:**
- Lagos as Federal Capital (1914-1991)
- Reasons for moving the capital
- Selection of Abuja
- Development of Abuja
- Abuja as FCT

**Behavioral Objectives:**
By the end of this lesson, pupils should be able to:
- State that Lagos was Nigeria's capital from 1914 to 1991
- Explain why the capital was moved from Lagos
- Name Abuja as the current Federal Capital Territory
- Describe how Abuja was developed

**Reference Materials:** Textbooks

**Previous Knowledge:** Pupils know that Nigeria is divided into states and has a federal government

**Presentation:**
- Step 1: Educator asks pupils what city houses the president's office
- Step 2: Educator explains that Lagos was the capital from 1914 to 1991
- Step 3: Educator discusses reasons for moving the capital
- Step 4: Educator explains how Abuja was selected and developed
- Step 5: Educator describes Abuja as the Federal Capital Territory

**Instructional Materials:** Pictures of Lagos and Abuja, map of Nigeria

**Evaluation:**
CLASSWORK: Why was Lagos no longer suitable as the capital of Nigeria?
