# Lesson Notes

**Class:** Grade 4
**Subject:** Social Studies
**Topic:** Globalization and International Relations II

## Lesson Content
Today, we will learn about organizations that bring countries together around the world and in Africa. These organizations help countries work in peace, protect people's rights, and develop together.

## United Nations (UN)
The United Nations is a big group of almost all the countries in the world. It was formed after World War II to keep peace around the world.

The UN has special agencies that do different work:

- UNICEF: Helps children all over the world get medicine, education, and food
- UNESCO: Helps protect schools and old buildings, and promotes education
- WHO: Helps countries fight diseases like malaria and flu

## African Union (AU)
The African Union is an organization made up of all African countries. They work together to solve problems, trade with each other, and promote peace in Africa.

Before, it was called the Organization of African Unity (OAU). They changed the name to African Union in 2002 to do more work together.

## ECOWAS
ECOWAS stands for Economic Community of West African States. It is a group of West African countries that work together in trade and peace.

Some countries in ECOWAS are Nigeria, Ghana, Ivory Coast, Senegal, and Togo. They help each other by sharing goods and keeping peace in the region.

## International Human Rights
Human rights are things that every person should have, no matter where they live. These include:

- The right to go to school
- The right to have food and medicine
- The right to live in peace
- The right to choose what to believe

## Global Citizenship
Being a global citizen means knowing that you are part of the whole world. It means respecting people from other countries and knowing that we all share the same planet Earth.

As global citizens, we should:
- Be kind to people from other countries
- Take care of our environment
- Help others in need, even if they are far away
