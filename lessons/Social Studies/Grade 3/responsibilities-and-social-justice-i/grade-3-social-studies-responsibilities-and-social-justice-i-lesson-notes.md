# Lesson Notes

**Class:** Grade 3
**Subject:** Social Studies
**Topic:** Responsibilities and Social Justice I

## Lesson Content
Today we learn about responsibility. Being responsible means doing the right thing without always being told. It means you can be trusted to take care of your tasks.

## Meaning of Responsibility
Responsibility is knowing what you should do and doing it well. When you are responsible, people can depend on you.

## Personal Responsibilities
Personal responsibilities are duties you have just for yourself:

- Keeping your body clean
- Getting up on time in the morning
- Eating your food
- Doing your homework

## Responsibilities to Family
Your family expects you to:

- Help with small chores at home
- Respect your parents and elders
- Share with your siblings
- Keep your room tidy

## Responsibilities to School
In school, you must:

- Listen to your teacher
- Do your classwork
- Keep the classroom clean
- Be kind to your classmates

## Responsibilities to Community
In your community, you should:

- Keep the environment clean
- Respect other people's belongings
- Help neighbors when they need it
- Follow community rules

## Being Accountable
Being accountable means you answer for what you do. If you break something, you should tell the truth. If you make a mistake, you should own it.

## Fulfilling Your Duties
To fulfill your duties:

- Always try your best
- Don't wait for someone to remind you
- Finish what you start
- Ask for help when you need it

## Assignment
Write two things you will do this week to show you are responsible at school.
