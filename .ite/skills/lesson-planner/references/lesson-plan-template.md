# Lesson Plan

**Class:** Grade 3

**Subject:** Social Studies

**Duration:** 40 minutes

**Gender:** Mixed

**Topic:** Prevention of Drug Abuse

**Subtopics:**
- Meaning of drugs
- Good and bad drugs
- Peer influence

**Behavioral Objectives:**
By the end of this lesson, pupils should be able to:
- Define drugs
- Identify good and bad drugs
- Explain who peers are
- State good and bad peer influence
- Know and demonstrate how to say no to the wrong influence

**Reference Materials:** Textbooks

**Previous Knowledge:** Pupils have seen and taken medicine before when sick

**Presentation:**
- Step 1: Educator asks if the pupils have taken medicine before and who gave it to them
- Step 2: Educator explains the meaning of drugs
- Step 3: Educator differentiates between good and bad drugs
- Step 4: Educator defines the meaning of peers and the differences between good and bad peer influence
- Step 5: Educator finishes by teaching the pupils how to say no and make good choices

**Instructional Materials:** Charts showing medicine and harmful drugs

**Evaluation:**
CLASSWORK: Drugs are ---- we take into our body.
